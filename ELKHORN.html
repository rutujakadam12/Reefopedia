🪸 Elkhorn Coral

📌 Overview

Elkhorn coral (Acropora palmata) is a major reef-building coral species found in the Caribbean and western Atlantic Ocean. It is named for its antler-like branches.

  • Fastest-growing coral species
  • Forms dense reef structures (thickets)
  • Protects coastlines
  • Critically endangered

👉 A foundation species supporting many marine organisms.

🌍 Habitat

📍 Location:

  • Caribbean Sea
  • Florida Keys
  • Bahamas
  • Gulf of Mexico

🌊 Conditions:

  • Shallow water (1–5 m)
  • Warm (25–29°C)
  • Clear water
  • High sunlight

🪸 Type: Reef crest zones

👉 Helps reduce wave energy and protect coasts.

🎯 Fun Facts

  • 🦌 Looks like elk horns
  • ⚡ Grows 5–10 cm per year
  • 🏠 Shelter for marine life
  • 🌞 Gets food from algae
  • 🧩 Can regrow from fragments
  • 🌊 Natural wave breaker

⚠️ Threats

  • 🌡️ Climate change → bleaching
  • 🦠 White Band Disease
  • 🌊 Ocean acidification
  • 🏭 Pollution
  • 🚤 Human damage
  • 🌪️ Storm destruction

👉 Population decline over 90% in some areas.

🌱 Conservation

  • 🧪 Coral nurseries & restoration
  • 🌊 Marine Protected Areas
  • 🔬 Research on resistant corals
  • 🌍 Climate action
  • 🧍 Public awareness programs
