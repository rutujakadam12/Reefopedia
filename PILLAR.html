🪸 Pillar Coral

📌 Overview

Pillar Coral (Dendrogyra cylindrus) is a unique coral that grows in tall vertical columns.

  • Only coral with pillar-like growth
  • Found in Caribbean region
  • Large visible polyps
  • Supports reef biodiversity

👉 Easily identifiable and ecologically important.

🌍 Habitat

📍 Location:

  • Caribbean Sea
  • Florida Keys
  • Gulf of Mexico

🌊 Conditions:

  • Depth: 1–20 m
  • Temperature: 25–29°C
  • Clear warm water

🪸 Type:

  • Reef flats
  • Slopes
  • Hard substrates

👉 Prefers stable shallow environments.

🎯 Fun Facts

  • 🪵 Vertical pillar structure
  • 👀 Polyps visible during day
  • 📏 Can grow over 2 meters
  • 🐠 Shelter for marine life
  • 🌞 Uses algae for energy

⚠️ Threats

  • 🌡️ Climate change (bleaching)
  • 🦠 SCTLD disease
  • 🌊 Ocean acidification
  • 🏭 Pollution
  • 🚤 Human damage
  • 📉 Severe population decline

🌱 Conservation

  • 🧪 Coral restoration
  • 🌊 Marine protected areas
  • 🔬 Research
  • 🌍 Climate action
  • 🧍 Awareness programs
