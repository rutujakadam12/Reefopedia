🪸 Black Coral

📌 Overview

Black coral belongs to the order Antipatharia. Though not always black outside, its skeleton is dark black.

  • Found in tropical oceans worldwide
  • Tree-like branching structure
  • Used in jewelry
  • Slow-growing and long-lived

👉 Provides habitat but does not form reefs.

🌍 Habitat

📍 Location:

  • Caribbean Sea
  • Pacific Ocean
  • Indian Ocean

🌊 Conditions:

  • Depth: 5–300+ m
  • Low light environments
  • Strong currents

🪸 Type:

  • Rocky surfaces
  • Caves
  • Steep reef slopes

👉 Adapted to deep, low-sunlight environments.

🎯 Fun Facts

  • 🖤 Black skeleton, colorful exterior
  • 💎 Used in jewelry
  • 🌳 Tree-like growth
  • 🐢 Lives hundreds to thousands of years
  • 🌌 Survives in deep ocean
  • 🍽️ Feeds on plankton

⚠️ Threats

  • 🪓 Overharvesting
  • 🌡️ Climate change
  • 🌊 Ocean acidification
  • 🏭 Pollution
  • 🚤 Deep-sea fishing damage

🌱 Conservation

  • 📜 Protected by CITES
  • 🌊 Marine protected areas
  • 🔬 Scientific research
  • 🧍 Awareness campaigns
