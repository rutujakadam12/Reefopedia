🪸 Acropora muricata

📌 Overview

Acropora muricata is a fast-growing staghorn coral with branching structures resembling deer antlers.

  • Belongs to genus Acropora
  • Forms dense coral thickets
  • Supports reef ecosystems
  • Highly sensitive to environment

👉 Plays a key role in reef biodiversity.

🌍 Habitat

📍 Location:

  • Indo-Pacific Ocean
  • Great Barrier Reef
  • Indian Ocean
  • Southeast Asia

🌊 Conditions:

  • Depth: 1–20 m
  • Temperature: 25–29°C
  • Clear, sunlit water

🪸 Type:

  • Reef flats
  • Lagoons
  • Branching colonies

👉 Requires high sunlight for photosynthesis.

🎯 Fun Facts

  • 🌿 Staghorn-like branches
  • ⚡ Grows 10–20 cm/year
  • 🐠 Shelter for marine life
  • 🧩 Can regrow from fragments
  • 🌞 Depends on algae

⚠️ Threats

  • 🌡️ Climate change (bleaching)
  • 🦠 Coral diseases
  • 🌊 Ocean acidification
  • 🌪️ Storm damage
  • 🚤 Human activities

🌱 Conservation

  • 🧪 Coral farming
  • 🌊 Marine protected areas
  • 🔬 Research
  • 🌍 Climate action
  • 🧍 Awareness programs
