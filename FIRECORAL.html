🪸 Fire Coral

📌 Overview

Fire coral (Millepora) is not a true coral but a hydrocoral related to jellyfish. It is known for its painful sting.

  • Belongs to class Hydrozoa
  • Has a hard calcium skeleton
  • Produces strong stings
  • Helps in reef building

👉 A unique organism that is both beautiful and dangerous.

🌍 Habitat

📍 Location:

  • Caribbean Sea
  • Indo-Pacific Ocean
  • Red Sea

🌊 Conditions:

  • Depth: 1–20 m
  • Temperature: 24–30°C
  • High sunlight

🪸 Type:

  • Reef crests
  • Lagoons
  • Rocky reef surfaces

👉 Found in wave-exposed areas to absorb energy.

🎯 Fun Facts

  • 🔥 Causes burning sting
  • 🪼 Related to jellyfish
  • 🌿 Uses algae for energy
  • 🧱 Strengthens reefs
  • 🟡 Yellow, brown or green
  • 🛡️ Uses stinging cells (nematocysts)

⚠️ Threats

  • 🌡️ Climate change (bleaching)
  • 🌊 Ocean acidification
  • 🏭 Pollution
  • 🚤 Human damage
  • 🌪️ Storm destruction

🌱 Conservation

  • 🌊 Marine protected areas
  • 🌍 Climate action
  • 🔬 Research
  • 🧍 Awareness programs
