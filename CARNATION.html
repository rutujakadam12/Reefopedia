🪸 Carnation Coral

📌 Overview

Carnation coral (Dendronephthya) is a soft coral known for its bright colors and flower-like shape.

  • Soft coral (no hard skeleton)
  • Found in Indo-Pacific oceans
  • Bright pink, red, orange, purple
  • Non-photosynthetic

👉 Depends on water currents for food.

🌍 Habitat

📍 Location:

  • Indo-Pacific Ocean
  • Red Sea
  • Southeast Asia

🌊 Conditions:

  • Depth: 10–100 m
  • Low-light areas
  • Strong currents

🪸 Type:

  • Reef slopes
  • Caves
  • Overhangs

👉 Thrives in flowing, nutrient-rich water.

🎯 Fun Facts

  • 🌺 Flower-like appearance
  • 🌈 Very bright colors
  • 🌌 No need for sunlight
  • 🍽️ Feeds on plankton
  • 🌊 Needs constant current
  • 🧱 Soft and flexible body

⚠️ Threats

  • 🌊 Pollution
  • 🌡️ Climate change
  • 🐠 Aquarium trade
  • 🚤 Human damage

🌱 Conservation

  • 🌊 Marine protected areas
  • 📜 Controlled collection
  • 🔬 Research
  • 🧍 Awareness programs
