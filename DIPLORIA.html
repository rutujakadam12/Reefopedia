🪸 Diploria Coral

📌 Overview

Diploria coral, commonly known as brain coral, is a reef-building coral with maze-like patterns resembling a human brain.

  • Belongs to genus Diploria
  • Found in Caribbean region
  • Includes Diploria labyrinthiformis
  • Supports reef formation

👉 Slow-growing but can live for centuries.

🌍 Habitat

📍 Location:

  • Caribbean Sea
  • Gulf of Mexico
  • Western Atlantic Ocean

🌊 Conditions:

  • Depth: 1–30 m
  • Temperature: 24–28°C
  • Clear, well-lit water

🪸 Type:

  • Reef flats
  • Lagoons
  • Fore-reefs

👉 Forms strong, stable reef structures.

🎯 Fun Facts

  • 🧠 Brain-like structure
  • 🐢 Lives for centuries
  • 🐌 Grows 0.5–1 cm/year
  • 🏗️ Builds strong skeletons
  • 🌞 Gets energy from algae
  • 🔁 Grooves increase surface area

⚠️ Threats

  • 🌡️ Climate change (bleaching)
  • 🦠 Black band & white plague diseases
  • 🌊 Ocean acidification
  • 🏭 Pollution
  • 🚤 Human damage
  • 🌪️ Storm damage

🌱 Conservation

  • 🧪 Coral nurseries
  • 🌊 Marine protected areas
  • 🔬 Scientific research
  • 🌍 Climate action
  • 🧍 Awareness programs
