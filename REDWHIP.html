🪸 Red Whip Coral

📌 Overview

Red whip coral (Ellisella spp.) is a soft gorgonian coral with a long, whip-like shape.

  • Belongs to octocorals
  • Long unbranched structure
  • Flexible and moves with currents
  • Provides habitat (not reef-building)

👉 Adapted to deep-water environments.

🌍 Habitat

📍 Location:

  • Indo-Pacific Ocean
  • Caribbean Sea
  • Red Sea

🌊 Conditions:

  • Depth: 10–100+ m
  • Low light
  • Moderate to strong currents

🪸 Type:

  • Reef slopes
  • Drop-offs
  • Deep reefs

👉 Grows vertically to capture food.

🎯 Fun Facts

  • 🪢 Whip-like structure
  • 🔴 Bright red color
  • 🌊 Bends without breaking
  • 🍽️ Feeds on plankton
  • 🐠 Hosts small fish
  • 🌌 Low/no photosynthesis

⚠️ Threats

  • 🪓 Overharvesting
  • 🌡️ Climate change
  • 🌊 Pollution
  • 🎣 Fishing damage
  • 🚤 Human activities

🌱 Conservation

  • 🌊 Marine protected areas
  • 📜 Trade regulations
  • 🔬 Research
  • 🧍 Awareness programs
