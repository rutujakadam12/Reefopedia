🪸 Great Star Coral

📌 Overview

Great Star Coral (Montastraea cavernosa) is a reef-building coral known for its star-shaped polyps.

  • Forms dome-shaped colonies
  • Supports reef stability
  • Large visible polyps
  • Highly adaptable species

👉 A resilient coral that survives in varied conditions.

🌍 Habitat

📍 Location:

  • Caribbean Sea
  • Gulf of Mexico
  • Western Atlantic Ocean

🌊 Conditions:

  • Depth: 1–100 m
  • Temperature: 24–29°C
  • High & low light tolerance

🪸 Type:

  • Reef crests
  • Slopes
  • Deep reefs

👉 One of the most adaptable coral species.

🎯 Fun Facts

  • ⭐ Star-shaped polyps
  • 🌙 Feeds at night
  • 🌞 Uses algae for energy
  • 🧱 Strong skeleton
  • 🐠 Habitat for marine life
  • 🎨 Green, brown, or orange

⚠️ Threats

  • 🌡️ Climate change (bleaching)
  • 🦠 Coral diseases
  • 🌊 Ocean acidification
  • 🏭 Pollution
  • 🚤 Human damage

🌱 Conservation

  • 🌊 Marine protected areas
  • 🧪 Coral restoration
  • 🔬 Research
  • 🌍 Climate action
  • 🧍 Awareness programs
